1. The calculator

Pick your programme (DP or CP), pick your year, pick your timezone if you know it. Then add each subject one at a time, enter the marks you have already sat, and choose a target grade.

For each subject you get a live readout showing what you need to score in remaining assessments to hit that target, plus a warning if the target is not mathematically possible.

At the end you get your predicted total, a university match list based on typical entry requirements, and a shareable link.

02
2. Grade boundaries

Every boundary on this site comes from the May 2025 IBDP grading report. 63 subjects are covered.

48 of those subjects have different boundaries for TZ1, TZ2, and TZ3. If you set a timezone in setup, the calculator uses the timezone-specific boundaries for those subjects. For subjects with no timezone variant (or if you leave the setting as Default), it uses the global boundaries.

03
3. Weighting formula

For each subject, your weighted percentage is calculated as:

  • SA1 = 10%
  • SA2 = 20%
  • SA3 = 20%
  • SA4 = 35%
  • IA = 15%

Your IA score (out of 20) is converted to a percentage before weighting. Missing assessments are treated as not yet sat and left out of the locked score.

04
4. The priority list

The priority tool ranks your subjects by how much grade improvement you get from the same amount of effort. It simulates a +10% improvement in SA4 for each subject and checks whether that extra effort crosses a grade boundary.

Subjects where a small SA4 push takes you up a full grade are high-leverage. Subjects where even a perfect SA4 would not change your grade are low-leverage. Study where the difference lives.

topic 4
05
5. The converter

The converter has two modes. Quick lookup lets you pick one subject, type a percentage, and instantly see your grade plus a full boundary table showing exactly how far you are from every grade.

Multi-subject lets you add several subjects at once and enter percentages for each. Useful when you want to sanity-check a whole report at once.

06
6. IBCP mode

If you pick CP in setup, the calculator switches to Career-related Programme rules. You pick 2 to 4 DP subjects, there is no 3-HL requirement, and instead of TOK + EE you grade your Reflective Project A to E.

Your total is calculated out of your subject count multiplied by 7. The CP Certificate requirements are shown at the end with a check on your Reflective Project grade.

7. If something looks wrong

If a grade boundary looks off, a calculation disagrees with what your teacher said, or a subject is missing, the fastest way to flag it is through the socials page. Include the subject name, the level, and the timezone if relevant.
